Let's talk about what we believe!

The things we believe go all the way back to the beginnings of history. Our beliefs are based on the timeless truth of the Bible, the revealed written word of God Himself. The Bible tells us that this Word of God cannot be broken (John 10:35). Because of this, the promises of God were fulfilled not just throughout history (Joshua 23:14), but also in Jesus Christ (2 Corinthians 1:20). And there's so much more about the Bible—like how God has imbued it with a spiritual power (Hebrews 4:12) that can change you from someone who is wounded and lonely into a new person inside! Our faith, in a nutshell

Five hundred years ago, the pioneers of our faith laid out five principles. We call them the Five Solas, because in the original Latin, the name of each principle began with the word Sola or a form of it, which means "Alone". Here they are:

Sola Scriptura—Scripture Alone

The Bible, being the Word of God (2 Peter 1:19-21), is the ultimate authority (Luke 4:4), not the traditions of the church or the opinions of men (Matthew 4:4, John 17:17).

Sola Gratia—Grace Alone

Salvation is by God's grace alone (Ephesians 2:4-9). Getting to heaven does not depend upon how well we live. God chose to save Jacob and reject Esau before they were born (Romans 9:11-13).

Sola Fide—Faith Alone

We are justified by faith alone, apart from our works (Romans 3:28, Philippians 1:29). Through faith alone we receive Christ and His perfect righteousness, and are thereby granted eternal pardon.

Solus Christus—Christ Alone

God saves us because of Christ alone, and not because of anything we do (Ephesians 2:8-10). Just as all men are born sinners because of Adam's sin, so All believers receive eternal pardon because of Christ's blood atonement on the cross. Everything we do is simply a response of gratitude to God for saving us through Christ (Romans 1:21, Joshua 24:14).

Soli Deo Gloria—For the Glory of God Alone

God alone deserves all the glory and the praise for the salvation of His people (Philippians 2:13). If man's salvation depends ultimately upon man's choice, then man deserves the glory and praise. We bring praise to God by serving Him according to His Word and by the power of His Spirit (Ephesians 5:8-9).
