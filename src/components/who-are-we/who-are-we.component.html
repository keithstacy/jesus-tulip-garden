Who We Are
Our Mission
Our mission is simple: to glorify God and to share the good news of His grace, saving sinners like us, through Jesus Christ. As the Bible says in Acts 4:12, "And there is salvation in no one else, for there is no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved."
Our Vision
Our vision is multifaceted. We pray that:
Each one of us makes God's word - The Bible - the focus of our living and decision-making. Psalm 19:7-11; 2 Timothy 3:16
We effectively reach into our community as individuals and as a church. Matthew 5:13-16; 1 Peter 2:9-12
Our Values
Our core values—what we call the five Solas—are simply five points that detail our Reformed teaching on things like:
  • The Bible
  • Faith
  • Grace
  • Jesus
  • Life's meaning
These ancient teachings find their roots in the Gospel message. They were rediscovered during the medieval Reformation, and continue to be more than just history— they are living truths to be received and embraced.
They remind us of what is central to Scripture and what is critically important for faithful, Bible-based churches to teach, preach, and live out before the watching world.
You can find out more about the five Solas here.
Giving
We teach that when someone joins our church family, they should pray carefully about what they should regularly give to support the work of God here at St. Paul's. But we don't ask our visitors to contribute unless they want to. And for those who do belong to our family, we trust them to do what God calls them to do—we don't check up on them, and we only track what members give so they can claim the donations for tax purposes. But we do seek to share what the Bible teaches about giving, because it is an important subject that God talks about a lot in the Scripture.
